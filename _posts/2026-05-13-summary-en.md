## [Xiaomi OneVL: One-Step Latent Reasoning Framework Open-Sourced](https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/7po3r6YtmuXm8Xny1bw61Q) ⭐️ 9.0/10

Xiaomi has released Xiaomi OneVL, a one-step latent-space reasoning framework that unifies Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models and world models for autonomous driving, achieving state-of-the-art results with a latency of 0.24 seconds. The framework is fully open-sourced, including model weights, training, and inference code. OneVL is the first framework to unify VLA and world models in autonomous driving, achieving a 5.4% latency of autoregressive methods while surpassing explicit chain-of-thought reasoning. Its full open-source release could accelerate research and development in efficient, interpretable autonomous driving systems. OneVL uses visual latent tokens to encode physical causal structures and language latent tokens for driving intent, with dual auxiliary decoders for future frame prediction and readable chain-of-thought during training, which are discarded during inference. It achieves a PDM-score of 88.84 on NAVSIM, the first latent reasoning method to exceed explicit CoT (88.29).

telegram · zaihuapd · May 13, 10:33

**Background**: Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models combine visual perception, language understanding, and action generation for autonomous driving, while world models predict future states. Traditional VLA methods use autoregressive chain-of-thought reasoning, which is slow. OneVL introduces latent-space reasoning, where reasoning happens in a compressed token space, enabling one-step parallel generation and significantly reducing latency.

## [Samsung union strike cuts chip output 58% in foundry](https://t.me/zaihuapd/41355) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Samsung Electronics' largest union reported that chip production dropped 58% for foundry and 18% for memory during the night shift on Thursday due to a massive walkout over wage disputes. This disruption at a global semiconductor leader threatens the already tight supply chain for memory and foundry chips, potentially impacting industries from smartphones to AI accelerators. The union has issued an ultimatum: if management does not concede, a full-scale 18-day strike will begin on May 21, which could severely disrupt global semiconductor supply.

The dispute centers on demands for higher base pay and removal of bonus caps.
