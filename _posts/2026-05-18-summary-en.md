## [Elon Musk Loses Lawsuit Against OpenAI and Sam Altman](https://techcrunch.com/2026/05/18/elon-musk-has-lost-his-lawsuit-against-sam-altman-and-openai/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A jury ruled against Elon Musk in his lawsuit against OpenAI and Sam Altman, finding that his claims were barred by the statute of limitations because he waited too long to file. This ruling sets a precedent for challenges to non-profit to for-profit transitions, potentially limiting future lawsuits on similar grounds and impacting AI governance debates. The jury found that Musk could have brought the same claims in 2019 or 2021, as the Microsoft deals in those years were similar to the 2023 deal central to his lawsuit.

hackernews · nycdatasci · May 18, 17:38 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48182754)

**Background**: Musk co-founded OpenAI as a non-profit in 2015 but left in 2018. He later sued, alleging OpenAI breached its non-profit mission by partnering with Microsoft and transitioning to a for-profit structure. The statute of limitations typically requires lawsuits to be filed within a certain period after the alleged harm is discovered.

**Discussion**: Commenters largely agreed with the verdict, noting Musk's claims were untimely and that he was aware of OpenAI's for-profit moves as early as 2019. Some expressed concern about the precedent for non-profit IP transfers to for-profits, suggesting government or taxpayer claims might still be possible.

## [Iran Launches Bitcoin-Backed Shipping Insurance for Hormuz](https://www.bloomberg.com/news/articles/2026-05-18/iran-starts-bitcoin-backed-shipping-insurance-for-hormuz-strait) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Iran has launched a Bitcoin-backed shipping insurance platform called Hormuz Safe for vessels transiting the Strait of Hormuz, with projected revenue exceeding $10 billion. This move could undermine US dollar dominance in global trade and challenge the US-led maritime security order, as Bitcoin enables Iran to bypass traditional financial sanctions. The insurance is settled in Bitcoin and targets cargo owners and shipping companies; it comes amid a crisis where Iran has largely blocked the strait since February 2026 following US-Israeli airstrikes.

hackernews · srameshc · May 18, 17:25 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48182592)

**Background**: The Strait of Hormuz is a critical maritime chokepoint for global oil trade. Since late February 2026, Iran has blocked most shipping through the strait in response to US-Israeli military actions, causing a major crisis. The US has doubled its ship reinsurance to $40 billion to keep oil flowing, while Iran now offers Bitcoin-backed insurance as an alternative.
