## [Report Exposes Chinese Grey Market for Claude API](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/chinese-grey-market-sells-claude-api-access-at-90-percent-off-through-proxy-networks-that-harvest-user-data) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A new report reveals that Chinese grey market API proxy services, known as 'transfer stations,' sell access to Anthropic's Claude models at up to 90% off official prices, but often substitute cheaper models and steal user data. This practice poses serious security and ethical risks for developers and businesses using these proxies, including data theft, model substitution, and potential intellectual property leakage, undermining trust in AI API ecosystems. The proxies cut costs by using stolen credit cards, bulk-registered accounts, or split subscriptions, and may even hire low-income individuals for identity verification. They also harvest user prompts and outputs for model distillation.

## [China's Top Court: Engagement Does Not Imply Sexual Consent](https://t.me/zaihuapd/41314) ⭐️ 8.0/10

China's Supreme People's Court has included the Shanxi 'engagement rape case' in its reference cases, clarifying that engagement does not imply sexual consent and that forced intercourse constitutes rape. This ruling reinforces women's sexual autonomy and corrects public misconceptions, setting a binding legal precedent for lower courts across China. The court emphasized that engagement carries no legal right to sexual intercourse, and that leaking information from closed-door trials will also be prosecuted.

**Background**: In China, the Supreme People's Court publishes reference cases to guide lower courts in adjudicating similar disputes. This case originated from a highly publicized incident in Datong, Shanxi, where a man was convicted of raping his fiancée after an engagement ceremony.
