## [AI Won't Speed Up Software Processes, Argues Engineer](https://frederickvanbrabant.com/blog/2026-05-15-i-dont-think-ai-will-make-your-processes-go-faster/) ⭐️ 8.0/10

A blog post argues that AI will not significantly accelerate software development processes because the primary bottleneck is clarifying vague requirements, not coding speed. This challenges the prevailing hype that AI will dramatically boost developer productivity, highlighting that the hardest part of software engineering remains understanding what to build. The author notes that even with infinitely fast typing, shipping useful products requires iteration and cross-functional collaboration to resolve product, technical, and organizational uncertainty.

hackernews · TheEdonian · May 17, 12:13 · [Discussion](https://news.ycombinator.com/item?id=48168221)

**Background**: Large Language Models (LLMs) like GPT-4 have been touted as tools that can generate code from natural language prompts, leading many to believe they will make software development faster. However, experienced developers know that coding is only a small part of the process; much time is spent understanding requirements, designing solutions, and coordinating with teams. This article pushes back against the narrative that AI will eliminate these challenges.

**Discussion**: Commenters largely agree, sharing anecdotes about vague requirements and emphasizing that AI still requires precise input. Some argue AI can help with ideation and documentation, but the core bottleneck remains requirement clarity.

## [GDS advises NHS to keep open source repos public](https://simonwillison.net/2026/May/17/gds-weighs-in/#atom-everything) ⭐️ 8.0/10

The UK Government Digital Service (GDS) published guidance on May 14, 2026, recommending that public sector organizations keep open source repositories public by default, countering the NHS's decision to close its repositories after vulnerabilities were reported via Project Glasswing. This public intervention by GDS signals a major policy disagreement within the UK government, emphasizing that open source benefits like cost savings and code reuse outweigh security risks, and could influence how other public sector bodies handle open source security. GDS's guidance, titled 'AI, open code and vulnerability risk in the public sector,' does not name the NHS directly but is widely interpreted as a rebuke. Terence Eden, a prominent blogger, characterized the move as a rare public escalation of internal civil service disagreements.

rss · Simon Willison · May 17, 15:59

**Background**: Project Glasswing is an Anthropic cybersecurity initiative launched in April 2026 that uses AI to find vulnerabilities in critical software. The NHS responded to reported vulnerabilities by closing its open source repositories, a move criticized by open source advocates. GDS is the UK government's digital center, responsible for setting digital standards across the public sector.

## [OpenClaw Developer Burns $1.3M in OpenAI API Tokens in a Month](https://www.tomshardware.com/tech-industry/artificial-intelligence/openclaw-creator-burns-through-1-3-million-in-openai-api-tokens-in-a-single-month) ⭐️ 8.0/10

Peter Steinberger, the developer of OpenClaw, revealed that his team spent $1.3 million on OpenAI API tokens in 30 days, consuming 603 billion tokens across 7.6 million requests from about 100 Codex agents. This case highlights the staggering real-world costs of running advanced AI agents at scale, with implications for the economics of AI-driven software development and the need for cost optimization strategies. The high cost was driven by Codex's 'fast mode' pricing; disabling it would have reduced the cost to about $300,000. The experiment used a version of GPT-5.5 dated April 23, 2026, and the costs were fully covered by OpenAI since Steinberger works there.

**Background**: OpenClaw is a platform for building and running AI agents, and Codex is OpenAI's model specialized for coding tasks. API tokens are units of text processed by the model, and pricing varies by model and mode. GPT-5.5 is OpenAI's latest frontier model for complex professional work, including agentic coding.
